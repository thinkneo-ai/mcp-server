## 1. Background and Purpose

1.1. The Controller has engaged ThinkNEO to provide AI orchestration, governance, and infrastructure services through the ThinkNEO platform, including but not limited to the Model Context Protocol (MCP) server, Agent-to-Agent (A2A) services, and related APIs (the "**Services**"), under a separate service agreement (the "**Principal Agreement**").

1.2. In providing the Services, ThinkNEO will Process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller. This DPA sets out the terms governing such Processing in accordance with applicable Data Protection Laws.

1.3. This DPA forms an integral part of the Principal Agreement. In the event of conflict between the Principal Agreement and this DPA in respect of Personal Data Processing, this DPA shall prevail.

---

## 2. Definitions

The following terms have the meanings given below. Capitalized terms not defined here have the meanings given in the Principal Agreement or applicable Data Protection Laws.

- **"Data Protection Laws"** means all laws and regulations applicable to the Processing of Personal Data, including but not limited to the EU General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679) ("**GDPR**"), the UK Data Protection Act 2018, the California Consumer Privacy Act ("**CCPA**") as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act, the Brazilian General Data Protection Law (Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados, "**LGPD**"), Hong Kong's Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance ("**PDPO**"), and any successor or equivalent legislation.
- **"Personal Data"**, **"Controller"**, **"Processor"**, **"Sub-processor"**, **"Data Subject"**, **"Processing"**, and **"Personal Data Breach"** have the meanings given in the GDPR.
- **"Standard Contractual Clauses"** or **"SCCs"** means the standard contractual clauses for the transfer of personal data to third countries adopted by the European Commission (Decision 2021/914), incorporated by reference into this DPA where applicable.
- **"Services"** means the ThinkNEO services described in §1.1.

## 10. Personal Data Breach Notification

10.1. ThinkNEO shall notify the Controller without undue delay, and in any event within **seventy-two (72) hours**, after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach affecting Personal Data Processed on behalf of the Controller.

10.2. The notification shall include, to the extent reasonably available at the time:
- a description of the nature of the Personal Data Breach, including the categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and records concerned;
- the likely consequences of the Personal Data Breach;
- the measures taken or proposed to address the Personal Data Breach, including measures to mitigate possible adverse effects;
- the contact point for further information.

10.3. ThinkNEO shall cooperate with the Controller and provide reasonable assistance with the Controller's notification obligations to supervisory authorities and Data Subjects.

10.4. ThinkNEO maintains a documented incident response process, including post-incident reports published in the Services trust documentation where appropriate (subject to confidentiality requirements).

- Be subject to confidentiality obligations.

## 14. Return or Deletion of Personal Data

14.1. Upon termination or expiration of the Principal Agreement, or upon the Controller's earlier written request, ThinkNEO shall, at the Controller's choice:
- delete all Personal Data Processed on behalf of the Controller, or
- return such Personal Data to the Controller in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format,

and delete existing copies, except to the extent that retention is required by applicable law.

14.2. The Controller may exercise this right via privacy@thinkneo.ai. ThinkNEO shall complete the requested action within thirty (30) days of receipt of the request and confirm completion in writing.

14.3. Backups containing Personal Data subject to a deletion request shall be deleted in line with ThinkNEO's documented backup retention schedule (currently: rolling 30-day retention, after which Personal Data in backups is overwritten or deleted).

15.1. ThinkNEO shall not Process Personal Data for its own purposes, except as strictly necessary to:
- Provide, maintain, monitor, and improve the Services;
- Detect, prevent, and mitigate security incidents, abuse, or fraud;
- Comply with legal obligations;
- Generate aggregated, anonymized, or de-identified data that no longer constitutes Personal Data.

15.2. ThinkNEO shall not use Personal Data to train AI/ML models, except where:
- Such use is explicitly opted into by the Controller in writing; or
- The data has been irreversibly anonymized such that it no longer qualifies as Personal Data under applicable Data Protection Laws.

# Annex B — Technical and Organizational Measures

ThinkNEO implements the following technical and organizational measures, in accordance with Article 32 GDPR. These measures are subject to ongoing review and improvement.

## B.1 Access Control

- **Authentication:** API key-based authentication for all programmatic access; multi-factor authentication for administrative access to infrastructure.
- **Authorization:** Role-based access control; least-privilege principle applied to internal access.
- **Least Privilege at Database Level:** Application database users have non-superuser, non-bypass-RLS roles. Superuser access is restricted to designated administrative accounts.
- **Key Management:** API keys are hashed at rest using industry-standard algorithms (Argon2id or equivalent). Plaintext keys are never stored.

## B.2 Encryption

- **Encryption in Transit:** All network communications with the Services use TLS 1.2 or higher.
- **Encryption at Rest:** Personal Data stored at rest is protected using disk-level encryption provided by the underlying hosting infrastructure (DigitalOcean encrypted volumes).
- **Certificate Management:** TLS certificates are issued by Let's Encrypt and pinned via DNS CAA records to prevent unauthorized issuance.

## B.3 Network and Infrastructure Security

- **Network segmentation:** Containerized services with explicit network policies between components.
- **Resource limits:** All containers run with explicit CPU and memory limits to prevent resource exhaustion.
- **Container security:** Where supported by the underlying image, containers run as non-root users.
- **Reverse proxy:** Inbound traffic is terminated at a reverse proxy (Caddy/nginx) with strict transport security (HSTS), modern cipher suites, and rate limiting.
- **DNS security:** DNS records are protected by DNSSEC where supported by the registrar; CAA, SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records are configured for the primary domain.

## B.4 Logging and Monitoring

- **Audit Logging:** All security-relevant events (authentication, authorization decisions, configuration changes, data access at administrative level) are logged.
- **Append-only audit trail:** Audit logs are protected against modification at the database trigger level (DELETE blocked).
- **Hash-chaining:** Audit log records are hash-chained to enable tamper detection.
- **Telemetry:** OpenTelemetry-based instrumentation for distributed tracing of requests across the Services.
- **Disk space monitoring:** Hourly automated monitoring with alerting at 80% utilization.

## B.5 Backup and Recovery

- **Backup frequency:** Daily automated backups of all Personal Data stores.
- **Backup scope:** Both containerized and host-level databases are backed up.
- **Retention:** Rolling 30-day retention.
- **Restore testing:** Weekly automated restore test to validate backup integrity and recovery procedures.
- **Documented Recovery Objectives:**
  - **Recovery Time Objective (RTO):** ≤ 4 hours for primary services.
  - **Recovery Point Objective (RPO):** ≤ 24 hours.

## B.6 Resilience and Reliability

- **Resource limits:** Per-container CPU and memory limits enforced.
- **Connection pooling:** Database connections are pooled to prevent resource exhaustion.
- **Circuit breakers:** Critical external dependencies (databases, caches, external APIs) are protected by circuit breakers to fail fast under sustained outages.
- **Chaos engineering:** Periodic automated chaos testing of failure scenarios.

## B.7 Application Security

- **Dependency management:** Dependencies are version-pinned and reviewed for known vulnerabilities.
- **Secret scanning:** Pre-commit hooks and CI/CD pipeline gates scan for accidentally committed secrets (gitleaks).
- **Input validation:** Guardrails detect and reject content patterns associated with prompt injection, cross-site scripting (XSS), and shell command injection.
- **Code review:** All production changes are committed to version control with traceable history.
- **Vulnerability management:** Dependency vulnerabilities are tracked and remediated according to severity.

## B.8 Personnel Security

- **Confidentiality:** All persons with access to Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations.
- **Background checks:** As ThinkNEO operates with minimal personnel, founder-level access is the primary access control. Future hires will be subject to background checks where legally permitted.
- **Awareness:** Security and privacy responsibilities are documented and reviewed.

## B.9 Incident Response

- **Documented incident response process:** Incidents are tracked from detection through containment, eradication, recovery, and post-mortem.
- **Notification timeline:** Personal Data Breach notification to Controllers within 72 hours of confirmation.
- **Post-incident reports:** Significant incidents are documented and (where appropriate and not subject to confidentiality concerns) made available to the Controller.

## B.10 Compliance Roadmap

- **SOC 2 Type I:** Readiness assessment in progress. Target: 2026.
- **ISO 27001:** Under evaluation for 2027.
- **Penetration testing:** Annual third-party penetration testing planned, with internal continuous testing in the interim.
